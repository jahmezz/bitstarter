As a young professional, I know the pain of the dreaded "errand day". The traffic jams, the waiting rooms and the phone calls can range from minor annoyance to frustrating timesink. No one likes to do errands, but technology really hasn't put a serious effort into making errands, appointments and busywork easier on the average person. In addition, I believe in this "right now" generation, there is a whole generation of people becoming adults in the next few years who will be very frustrated by how tedious it is to set up one's required tasks that day. I aim to change that with ErrandConnect. ErrandConnect acts as an aid and interface between people and their errands. The general description of this application is that it will be the overarching application for people's schedules and to-do lists. Here are some of the problems this application will solve.

One major problem in errands is having to set appointments. ErrandConnect seeks to solve this problem by acting as the communication and storage between customer and service. With ErrandConnect, people will be directly connected with their service providers. We aim to reach out to service providers such as dentists, doctors and other professionals so they will be connected to the application as well. If a doctor and his patient are connected with ErrandConnect, the customer can look directly at the doctor's schedule and suggest an appointment at a certain day and time. This time will be stored in his errand list as well as the doctor's. Instead of making a phone call, selecting a time and copying it down on his schedule, the customer now has done all of this at the same time with a click of a button on ErrandConnect.
